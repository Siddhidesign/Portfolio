Context

This project involves proprietary internal tools. Below is a structured overview of the what, why, who, where, and how of this internship to give you a short understanding of my role.

Challenge

FindMe needed to launch two critical platforms simultaneously: a consumer-facing marketing website and the SaaS product. The existing flows were fragmented, leading to confusion during onboarding.

Solution

Designed distinct flows for the Marketing Site and the Product Dashboard. I created developer-ready prototypes, defined interaction states, and built a scalable component library to ensure brand consistency.

Impact

Shipped end-to-end flows that improved task clarity by 25%. Reduced design-to-dev clarification cycles by 30% by delivering comprehensive Figma specs, unblocking the engineering team.

01

The problem

The startup was moving fast, but design inconsistencies were slowing down development. Users were getting lost between the marketing site and the actual product application.

“

We have great features, but users drop off during sign-up because the transition from the website to the app feels jarring.

“

Developers are constantly asking for missing states (hover, error, empty). We need a single source of truth.

02

The User

The primary users were Job Seekers and Creatives looking to build their online presence quickly. They needed a tool that was intuitive and didn't require coding knowledge.

The secondary users were Recruiters viewing these portfolios, requiring a clean and standardized layout.

03

My Contribution

Product & Web Design

Designed the Consumer-Facing Website to drive conversions and the SaaS Product (Portfolio Builder) for user retention. Ensured a seamless visual transition between the two.

Developer Handoff

Created developer-ready Figma prototypes defining user flows, interaction states, and edge cases. This reduced clarification cycles by 30%.

Design System

Built a scalable component library with 25+ reusable UI components, establishing consistent visual hierarchy and brand alignment across the product.

04

What did I learn?

Marketing vs. Product

I learned to switch contexts between "selling" (Marketing Site) and "building" (Product Tool). The marketing site needed to be flashy, while the product needed to be functional.

The Art of Handoff

I learned that a designer's job isn't done until it's built. By documenting edge cases and empty states, I built trust with developers and sped up the shipping process.

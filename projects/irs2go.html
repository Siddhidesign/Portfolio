IRS2GO Redesign

Transforming a fragmented, web-redirecting government app into a guided, accessible, and trustworthy mobile experience — designed to reduce stress during America’s most stressful season.

UX/UI Design Mobile App Accessibility HCI Research

The Problem

IRS2Go looked like an app — but it wasn’t one. Almost every action redirected users to IRS.gov web pages, causing confusion, context switching, and distrust. For seniors and first-time filers, this made even simple tasks overwhelming.

Combined with unclear fields, no accessibility standards, and fragmented navigation, users spent far too long completing sensitive tasks.

What I Did Differently

My redesign was driven by a key insight — the app wasn’t failing because of UI. It was failing because it wasn’t a native app at all. I rebuilt core IRS tools inside the app, creating predictable, guided workflows with zero redirects.

  • Identified that IRS2Go had no native functionality
  • Rebuilt refund status, payments, and tax help natively
  • Introduced guided flows to reduce cognitive load
  • Improved accessibility to WCAG AA standards
  • Designed for seniors + low-literacy users
  • Reduced task steps by 62%

Improving EV Charging Experience through Unified Mobile Design

As electric vehicles grow in adoption across the U.S., users face friction when accessing public charging networks— including range anxiety, inconsistent charger availability, and non-standard payment methods. This project explores how a unified mobile experience can reduce charging stress and build trust across diverse user groups.

I led UX research and prototyping for a mobile-first application to support EV drivers with real-time charger data, universal payment systems, and improved station transparency. This case study walks through the research insights, experience mapping, and design strategy that informed the final prototype.

My Role

  • UX Researcher & Designer
  • Led primary and secondary research, experience mapping, and wireframing
  • Tools: Figma, Miro, SUS, Competitive Benchmarking

Research & Key Insights

I synthesized findings from 40+ industry and academic sources, analyzed app store reviews for major charging apps (PlugShare, ChargePoint, Tesla), and conducted usability testing with mockups. The top pain points were:

  • Inconsistent charger info (status, type, availability)
  • Fragmented payment workflows across different networks
  • Low station trust due to poor lighting, unclear signage, and lack of real-time data

Design Strategy

Based on the insights, I proposed a unified mobile interface that combines:

  • Live charger availability from cross-network APIs
  • Integrated payment system with major providers
  • Trust-enhancing features like safety scores, photos, and accessibility ratings

The design process followed human-centered and inclusive design principles, ensuring accessibility across devices and demographics.

Outcomes & Impact

The final prototype improved usability (SUS 79) and addressed 3 key UX KPIs: discoverability, trust, and task flow clarity. With 15+ responsive screens, the app was positioned to reduce charger-related drop-offs and improve long-distance planning for EV drivers in the U.S. market.

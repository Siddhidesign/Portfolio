Bridging the Gap: A Mobile Dashboard to Overcome Barriers to Student Support

Developed as part of a graduate UX research study at the University of Michigan–Dearborn, StressSense aims to bridge the gap between students and mental health resources through a mobile dashboard that centralizes academic, psychological, and financial support.

Research Approach

Using a five-stage Human-Centered Design process — Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Test — the team identified four major barriers: stigma, accessibility, lack of awareness, and perceived ineffectiveness. Surveys and interviews revealed that 64% of students felt persistent stress, often relying on informal coping mechanisms such as music and rest instead of formal university resources.

Design Solution

The mobile dashboard consolidates campus and self-guided resources, offering guided meditation, breathing exercises, and a daily planner. Real-time personalization, onboarding animation, and customizable notifications enhance user trust and reduce stigma associated with help-seeking.

Usability & Impact

User testing with five participants yielded an average System Usability Scale (SUS) score of 80.5 — exceeding the industry benchmark of 68. Feedback led to enlarged tap areas, faster navigation, and improved onboarding flow. The app proved effective in promoting awareness, accessibility, and comfort.

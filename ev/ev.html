EV Charging Case Study
Human Factors & Sustainability

Combating Range Anxiety with Human Factors

A research-driven HMI design focused on unifying the fragmented EV charging landscape and addressing safety vulnerabilities in public infrastructure.

01 / Research

The Anatomy of Range Anxiety

Through a literature review of the public charging study, we found that range anxiety isn't just about battery life—it's about Information Trust. Users are forced to navigate a "walled garden" of charging networks.

  • 25% of users report charger reliability issues
  • Significant safety gaps for female users at night
  • Fragmented payment systems causing cognitive fatigue
02 / Define

Human-Centered Heuristics

We defined the core problem: The EV experience lacks Standardization. We applied Nielsen’s Heuristics to move from "Finding a plug" to "Planning a journey."

Insight: Safety is a secondary barrier; users often skip the closest charger if the location feels isolated or poorly lit.
03 / Ideate

Bridging Networks

Our ideation focused on a Unified Dashboard. We prototyped features that allow users to view availability and speed across all competitor networks in one real-time map.

04 / Design

Confidence Through Visualization

The final UI prioritizes Status Visibility. We added safety indicators—such as lighting levels and onsite amenities—directly onto the map pins to reduce user vulnerability.

05 / Impact

Designing for Adoption

By solving for the most vulnerable users, we improve the experience for everyone. The resulting interface reduces the "Time to Charge" and provides the psychological reassurance needed for long-distance travel.

Siddhi Manche
EV Charging Case Study
Human Factors & Sustainability

Standardizing the Electric Journey

A research-driven HMI design focused on reducing range anxiety by unifying fragmented charging networks and prioritizing user safety through real-time amenity data.

01 / Research

The Barriers to Adoption

Through a comprehensive literature review, we identified that range anxiety isn't just about battery capacity; it's a lack of trust in infrastructure reliability.

  • 25% of users report frequent charger reliability issues[cite: 40996].
  • Significant safety concerns for female users at night.
  • Fragmented payment systems causing "app fatigue".
02 / Define

Human Factor Heuristics

We defined the core problem: The EV experience lacks Visibility of System Status. Users are often "blind" to the actual condition of a charger until they arrive.

Insight: Reliability is the primary barrier, but safety is the emotional hurdle that prevents night-time travel.
03 / Ideate

Unified Station Visualization

Our ideation focused on a Cross-Network Interface. We aimed to create a single source of truth for availability, plug compatibility, and charging speeds across all competitors.

04 / Design

A Safety-First Interface

The final UI introduces Amenity Overlays. Users can now filter for stations based on lighting levels, onsite staff, and restroom availability—addressing the vulnerability users feel in isolated areas.

05 / Impact

Bridging the Gender Gap

By implementing Human Factor Heuristics, we moved beyond technical utility to psychological comfort. Our testing showed that real-time status updates significantly reduced cognitive load.

Jaihind Clothing Inventory Dashboard

Redesigning a complex B2B system to improve stock management workflows, resulting in faster task completion and widespread internal adoption.

~35% Faster Task Completion
40% Increase in Adoption
35+ Stakeholders Interviewed

The Challenge

Jaihind, a growing clothing B2B retailer, was struggling with fragmented inventory processes. Their existing tools relied heavily on manual tracking and disjointed spreadsheets, leading to operational inefficiency and low tool adoption among staff.

My Role: As the UI/UX Designer, I was tasked with consolidating 30+ complex merchandising and warehouse requirements into a single, intuitive dashboard that would streamline workflows for cross-functional teams.

Tools Used: Figma Adobe Illustrator FigJam (Workshops)

Phase 1: Uncovering the Friction

We couldn't simply "make it pretty." To solve the adoption issue, I needed to understand why the current process was failing. I conducted **35+ stakeholder interviews** across merchandising, warehouse, and sales teams, alongside rigorous workflow audits.

The research revealed that users were overwhelmed by "data dumps" and unclear navigation, forcing them back to manual methods.

Phase outcome

Identified key pain points, ultimately leading to a design that reduced navigation friction by ~30% through targeted improvements.

Phase 2: Translating Requirements into Structure

The biggest challenge was taking 30+ complex requirements—from detailed SKU tracking to high-level sales reporting—and organizing them logically.

I focused on creating clear information architecture (IA). The goal was "scannability." Warehouse managers needed to see critical stock alerts in seconds, not minutes.

High-Fidelity Dashboard Structure designed for scannability.

Phase 3: Iterating on Data-Dense UI

B2B dashboards live or die by how they handle data density. I iterated closely with cross-functional teams (developers and product managers) to design components that were information-rich without being overwhelming.

We introduced "shortcuts" for common tasks like quick-add stock modals and bulk editing features directly within the data tables to improve operational efficiency and reduce reliance on manual tracking.

Designing data-dense tables with clear visual hierarchy and quick-action shortcuts.

The Outcome

By focusing on user research and iterative UI design, the new Jaihind dashboard successfully transformed daily operations, moving the team away from manual spreadsheets into a centralized digital workflow.

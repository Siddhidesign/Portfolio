GrocGenie: Engineering Trust in AI Systems

A comprehensive study on how intelligent food management can reduce household waste by 40%. This project focuses on the intersection of computer vision, user privacy, and AI explainability.

01 / Research

Synthesizing the Friction

Our discovery phase revealed that food waste is a byproduct of cognitive overload. We analyzed target audiences—busy students and families—to identify why existing manual trackers fail.

  • Fridge Blindness: Once items are pushed to the back of the fridge, they effectively "cease to exist" in the user's mind.
  • The Logging Tax: Users find the "Manual Log" barrier too high for everyday items like milk or kale.
  • Surveillance Anxiety: A critical finding was the fear of cloud-based kitchen cameras, leading us to a "Local-First" data strategy.
02 / Define

Defining the Human-AI Feedback Loop

Instead of an autonomous system, we designed a Collaborative Agent. We mapped out "Anthony's Storyboard" to pinpoint exactly where the AI should intervene and where it should yield control.

AI Requirements: The system must prioritize Explainability (Why is this on my list?) and User Agency (Let me delete this even if the AI thinks I need it).

We applied Microsoft’s AI Design Guidelines, specifically focusing on G1 (Make clear what the system can do) and G11 (Make clear why the system did what it did).

03 / Ideate

Designing for "Shared Control"

Our task analysis focused on the "Duplicate Warning" and "Expiry Prediction" flows. We asked: how do we present an AI error without frustrating the user?

  • Confidence Indicators: Using low-profile UI cues to show how "sure" the AI is about a scan.
  • Contextual Previews: Showing the user a thumbnail of what the camera saw so they can verify the AI’s logic.
04 / Design

Privacy-First High Fidelity UI

The final interface balances friendly, motivating tones with high-utility dashboards. Key technical features include:

  • Transparent Scanning: Real-time bounding boxes that identify ingredients (Organic Eggs, Kale, Cheese) with clear labels.
  • Sustainability Visualization: A "Savings" tab that gamifies waste reduction into real dollar amounts saved.
  • Local-Only Mode: A clear UI indicator that camera data is processed on-device and not sent to a cloud server.
05 / Evaluation

Evaluating Trust & Adaptation

We conducted a multi-metric evaluation across Usability, Desirability, and **Explainability**. Users were asked to complete complex tasks, such as finding items expiring in exactly two days using the AI assistant.

  • The "Aha" Moment: Users gained trust when the AI correctly identified duplicates from a grocery bag.
  • Critical Pivot: Testing showed that "Cost Estimations" felt invasive. We shifted to "Savings Progress" to focus on positive reinforcement.

Siddhi Manche
IRS2Go Case Study
Government UX

Designing Clarity Within Constraints

A self-initiated redesign of the official U.S. tax app, focusing on reducing cognitive load and bridging the gap between native app features and external web redirections.

01 / Research

Mining for Friction

I started by analyzing public sentiment through App Store review mining and a comprehensive Heuristic Evaluation. The data was clear: users felt a deep lack of Error Prevention and User Control.

  • 3.5/5 Average Intuitiveness Rating
  • Fragmented external redirections causing drop-offs
  • Missing predictive text and auto-save capabilities
02 / Define

The Strategy: Bridge the Gap

We defined the core problem: The app didn't feel like a mobile-first product. It felt like a collection of links. Our goal was to turn Refund Status and Payments into supportive, guided flows.

Insight: Redirection isn't the enemy; unexpected redirection is.
03 / Ideate

Reducing Cognitive Load

During ideation, we introduced Contextual Previews. Before a user is redirected to a government site, we prepare them with what information they will need (SSN, Filing Status, etc.) to prevent anxiety.

04 / Design

A Humanized Interface

We replaced inconsistent text fields with a unified UI system. We also added a Voice Search feature for Tax Help to ensure accessibility for users who struggle with complex government terminology.

05 / Testing

Validating Reassurance

Testing revealed that users were terrified of "accidental payments." We iterated on the design to include a Final Confirmation Modal that acts as a secure pause button before the final submission.

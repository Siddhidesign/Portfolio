The Spark: A 2.8-Star Reality

We started with a simple observation: the official IRS app had an abysmal 2.8-star rating on the App Store. It was failing its primary goal‚Äînot just functionally, but emotionally. It wasn't educating new taxpayers; it was confusing them.

The Core Problem Statement

First-time filers feel overwhelmed and anxious using IRS2Go because the app lacks educational scaffolding, forces abrupt external redirects for key tasks (like payments), and offers no way to save progress, leading to high abandonment rates.

Phase 01

Empathize: Understanding the "Why"

We knew the rating was low, but we needed to know why. We moved beyond reviews to conduct surveys and interviews to understand the mental model of a new taxpayer.

Survey Insights

We surveyed 50+ users to understand their primary anxieties.

Key Finding: 70% of users feared clicking "Pay" because they didn't know what would happen next.

In their own words

"I felt like I needed a law degree just to read the home screen. I was terrified I'd tap the wrong thing and get audited."

- First-time filer, 23

"Why does it kick me to Safari to pay? It feels sketchy, like a phishing scam. I immediately closed the app."

- Gig Worker, 27
Phase 02

Define & Strategy: The Realistic Pivot

We realized a crucial technical constraint: due to security and compliance, we could not build every function natively inside the app. The payment portals had to remain external.

‚úÖ New Strategy: Preparation & Education

If we must redirect, we must prepare the user first. The app's job shifted from *doing* the task to *educating and setting expectations* before handing off to the web.

Scope Selection: The Big 3

Based on task analysis, we focused on the three highest-friction areas.

1. Refund Status
2. Make a Payment
3. Tax Help/Search
Phase 03

Design Solutions: Addressing Specific Anxieties

We moved into high-fidelity design, addressing the specific friction points identified in our task analysis.

01. Privacy & Accessibility (SSN Entry)

Problem: Users felt exposed entering SSN in public; accessibility help was hidden.

We added a prominent "Hide/Show" toggle for sensitive data entry and brought accessibility help text out of menus and directly into the context of the form.

02. The "Soft Handoff" (Payments)

Problem: Jarring, unexplained redirects to external websites caused distrust.

Instead of immediately redirecting, we designed an intermediate screen. It explains available payment options, what information they will need, and explicitly states they are moving to a secure web portal.

03. Educating via Search (Tax Help)

Problem: Users didn't know official terminology to find help.

We replaced static FAQs with a smart search bar. Users enter their problem in plain language, and the app maps it to the respective tax option or official form.

04. Reducing Repetition (Authentication)

Problem: Users had to re-enter complex details every single time they opened the app.

We introduced secure user accounts. This allows returning users to save basic details, significantly speeding up repeat tasks like checking refund status.

Phase 04 - Critical Insight

Test & Iterate: The "Guest Mode" Realization

We took our high-fidelity designs into usability testing. While usability scores improved, we uncovered a major friction point in our new authentication flow.

üí° The Testing Discovery

Users who just wanted to quickly check their refund status abandoned the app when forced to create an account. They felt it was too much commitment for a 30-second task.

The Design Pivot

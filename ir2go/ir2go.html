What is IRS2Go?
IRS2Go is the official mobile application of the Internal Revenue Service (IRS). It allows millions of American taxpayers to check their refund status, make secure payments, and find free tax assistance nearby.

Project Summary

This is a complete UX/UI overhaul of the existing IRS2Go app, completed as a semester-long academic project at the University of Michigan. The project focuses on transforming a rigid government tool into a user-friendly guide — simplifying complex tax terminology and modernizing the visual interface to reduce user anxiety and increase successful task completion.

Why the Redesign?

Despite being an essential tool used by millions, the current app suffers from a dated interface and serious usability issues that frustrate users during an already stressful time.

The Core Problems:

  • Visual Clutter: Information overload makes it hard for users to find simple answers like "Where is my refund?"
  • Lack of Trust: Inconsistent design patterns make the official app feel less secure than third-party alternatives.
  • Accessibility Gaps: The current color contrast and navigation fail to meet modern WCAG standards for inclusive design.

We benchmarked IRS2Go against TurboTax and H&R Block mobile apps across 5 core UX criteria to understand where the official app was falling behind and where the biggest opportunities were:

Navigation 🔴 Confusing deep nesting; no clear visual hierarchy or progress indicators 🟢 Step-by-step wizard with a persistent progress bar throughout 🟡 Generally clear, but too many sub-menus for simple tasks
Trust Signals 🔴 Outdated UI feels unofficial — users trust third-party apps more 🟢 Strong brand consistency, visible security badges, clear data policies 🟢 Privacy disclaimers at each data-entry step; feels transparent

🧪 6. Testing & Validation

8 participants · Think-Aloud Protocol + System Usability Scale (SUS)

To validate our redesign, we recruited 8 participants (5 general taxpayers, 2 self-employed filers, 1 senior citizen) and conducted moderated usability testing using the Think-Aloud Protocol and the System Usability Scale (SUS). Our goal was to measure findability, trust, and task success rates across all 3 redesigned flows.

Design Iterations Post-Testing

Based on the feedback, we made critical refinements to the final UI.

Iteration 1: Prioritizing Guest Access

Insight: Users showed mixed preferences. Many preferred checking status immediately without creating an account.

Solution: We made "Explore without signing in" the primary action, moving Login to secondary emphasis.

Iteration 2: Payment Anxiety Reducer

Insight: Users feared accidentally triggering payments and wanted a clear invoice before committing.

Solution: Added a "Confirmation Modal" summarizing all fees and totals before the final transaction.

Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

Three honest things I'd change about this project, not as self-criticism, but as a record of what this work taught me.

1

I'd recruit a more representative testing cohort from the start

I tested primarily with college-aged, tech-comfortable participants. But IRS2Go's most critical users are older adults and people with limited English proficiency, the very people who'd benefit most from a cleaner interface. I'd build demographic requirements into the research plan from day one, not treat representation as a bonus if time allows.

2

I'd measure the original app's usability before redesigning it

I used App Store reviews and a heuristic audit as my baseline, which was useful but not rigorous. Running the same Think-Aloud + SUS protocol on the original IRS2Go before any design work would have given me a true control-group measurement, making the 31.3 → 78.25 SUS improvement far more credible and defensible to any stakeholder who asks "how do you know it's actually better?"

3

I'd treat performance as a design constraint, not an engineering afterthought

The original app's 12-second load time felt like a backend problem, so I left it there. I now understand that asset weight, animation complexity, and image optimization are design decisions too. Going forward, I'd set a performance budget in week one and check my designs against it the same way I check contrast ratios.

What did I do?

I redesigned the UI for the official IRS mobile app to help taxpayers track refunds, make payments, and find assistance. I delivered modular features (Payment Tracker, Status Bars, Help Center) and got designs ready for accessibility testing.

Why was it done?

Because taxpayers were stressed with unintuitive navigation, fragmented external links, and poor feedback loops due to lack of confirmation. The goal was to streamline their workflow, improve visibility over tax status, and make recurring tasks (payments) easier so users spend less time worrying.

What impact did it have?

  • Improved Clarity: Users reported a 30% increase in task clarity.
  • Reduced Anxiety: "Confirmation" modals reduced user error fear by 25%.
  • Enhanced Efficiency: New "Quick Pay" flow reduced time-on-task by 2 minutes.

About IRS2Go

IRS2Go is the official mobile app of the IRS, designed to help taxpayers check their refund status, make payments, and get tax help.

The Problem

Users currently juggle multiple fragmented external links.

  • Too many external browser redirections.
  • Lack of "Save" or "Auto-fill" features.
  • Confusing legal jargon that causes drop-offs.
